Last Summer

by Rizky Azmi Swandy



Just a random thought.

Our country is well-known as a really humble, togetherness, and “family-oriented” base social culture. Even if we just walked past someone’s house, there always be some people who offer us to come by their house, even though they don’t mean it. Kinda funny. Instead, people in the first-world country tend to be self-oriented or more likely to not care so much about other businesses. But, let me take you to one thing that I’ve been thinking about lately.

If we see at the transportation culture in first-world countries, let’s see a closer example. When I was in DC and Baltimore last summer, It was really different. You can actually go anywhere by subway. I observed some things there and found something interesting about the transportation culture. Even if they have different interests, maybe that person wanna go to their friend’s house, or the other person wanna go to the office, or just like us, we headed to Kedubes RI in DC, and they’re heading in the same direction, we were heading the same direction like others, same line. It’s like they have actually built a really well-designed culture to also built their mindset. Philosophically, it’s like they’re saying, “Even if we have different interests, we have to find a common vision and go through that, then we can fulfill our own interests”. Conversely here in our country, we tend to use our own transportation, car, motorcycle, anything. We even have a decacorn startup company, based on single transportation use, GoJek. (Cont./)



People are just tryna fulfill their own interests. Well, it’s kinda sad that we don’t have good public transportation nowadays. Pardon me, we have it, but only in Jakarta.

I was scrolling through my Twitter timeline and saw an interesting tweet. So, there’s a tweet about a girl that said, “A man should have a car to date me, if they don’t, then we should just have a video call at home, or just don’t date any girl, LOL”. Replies have gone wild. But, there’s one thing that came up to my mind. That’s not wrong actually to demand comfort, there’s nothing wrong with it in my opinion. The problem is with our public transportation. If we have comfortable public transportation, walkable sidewalk, and many green open spaces, we don’t have to think or even worry about what or how are we gonna go on a date with someone. We just go by subway, bus, or anything and go anywhere.
